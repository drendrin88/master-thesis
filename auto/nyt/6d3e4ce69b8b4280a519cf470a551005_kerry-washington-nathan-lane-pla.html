Kerry Washington, Nathan Lane and Others Read Plays About Our Future

We asked 15 playwrights to create original works around their visions of America in 2024. Six of them were read onscreen.

April 18, 2019

What will America be like in 2024? That was the prompt for the 15 playwrights who created original works for T’s recent Culture issue. Paul Rudnick scripted Jared and Ivanka’s possible debate over which one of them should be the next president, Jackie Sibblies Drury imagined love in the time of paranoia — and Jeremy O. Harris delivered a monologue, in Auto-Tune, about a certain performer’s state of mind. Below you’ll find these plays and more, performed by casts that include Nathan Lane, Kerry Washington and John Lithgow.
