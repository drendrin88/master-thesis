Relais pour la Vie: the 25-hour relay race against cancer

Runners take part in the race through the night to raise awareness and funds

rosa clemente, translated by hannah brenton
24.03.2019

More than 12,000 runners took part in the fourteenth edition of Relais pour la Vie, or 'Relay for Life', at the Coque sports centre on Saturday to raise money for the fight against cancer.

The relay race, which continues non-stop for 25 hours, was still going strong on Sunday as participants showed their support for cancer patients and their families.

The race was an hour longer than usual this year, instead of the traditional 24-hour commitment, to commemorate 25 years of the Cancer Foundation, which set up the event in Luxembourg.

The mood was emotional, but also relaxed, as the runners made their way round the track.

The event will come to a close at 8:30pm on Sunday.
