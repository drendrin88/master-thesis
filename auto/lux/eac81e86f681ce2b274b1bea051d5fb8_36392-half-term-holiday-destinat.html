Half-term holiday destinations near Luxembourg

Luxembourg Times guide to half-term destinations easily accessible from Luxembourg

sarita rao
03.02.2019

Though distant and sunny shores may beckon, you’d be surprised at the number of hidden gems closer to home that are perfect for keeping the kids entertained during a half-term holiday, rain, shine or snow.

Strasbourg

Home to the European Parliament for six months of the year, Strasbourg is a great place for a short break. The city’s Cathedrale de Nôtre Dame soars over the old town and kids will love its famous astronomical clock. Nearby Place Kléber, with its water fountains, is a good traffic-free space for kids to run about while you stop for lunch.

If you like a bit of history, then take a stroll around Petite France’s narrow canals flanked by half-timbered houses, try an Alsatian delicacy or sit in a cosy beer snug. Children can run off some steam in the Parc de l’Orangerie , which boasts several play areas, a lake where you can rent boats, a free mini-zoo with flamingos, monkeys, goats and the ubiquitous stork; the symbol of Strasbourg.

Strasbourg has a great tram system, and a 24 hour ‘trio’ ticket provides unlimited travel for three people costing less than 10 Euros. Children under 5 travel free.

Where to stay

If you prefer the comforts of a hotel then the Mercure provides great family rooms, an indoor swimming pool and is close to a tram stop. Le Jean-Sébastien Bach hotel has family rooms and aparthotels, in addition to free parking, and is located close to the Parc de l’Orangerie.

Maastricht & surrounds

Explore the cobbled streets of Maastricht and the lovely Vrijthof Square, or browse for some books in the nearby Selexyz Dominicanen Bookstore – a 13th century Dominican church converted into an impressive contemporary bookstore which The Guardian called the “best bookstore in the world”.

Take a quiet moment in the Basilica of Saint Servatius or light a candle in the Basilica of Our Lady, walk around the fortifications to spot Hell’s Gate built over 800 years ago. There’s also a Natural History Museum which contains the remains of dinosaurs and giant turtles, an aquarium and a working beehive. A number of bicycle rental companies do city tours (and have family bikes)

Just outside the city you can visit the town of Valkenberg with its famous castle, velvet caves, cable car, and year-round toboggan ride.

Where to stay

There are several main brand hotels like Novotel and Crown Plaza, but if you’re looking for a kid-friendly place the Townhouse design hotel gets good reviews. Narvik Home Parc’s Mooi Zutendaal has lovely log cabins which are perfect for families, in a complex complete with nature trails, an indoor pool and play areas. Landal’s Domein de Cauberg offers self-catering cottages and flats. Both destinations are less than 30 minutes drive to Maastricht.

Dijon

A bit further afield, but full of charm, Dijon makes a great family break. Perhaps seen as more of a summer destination there is however still lots to enjoy in the winter months, with the added bonus of a string of beautiful historic villages and towns nearby.

The wonderful  Musee de la vie Bourguignonne (free entry) has life-size displays of what the streets, shops and even a family kitchen might have looked like in Burgundy a few hundred years ago. There are plenty of hands-on activities to keep children entertained, such as dressing up in 19th century clothes or colouring in pictures of one of the display scenes. For lunch you can taste the regions signature dish, Boeuf Bourguignon, while the kids run around the fountain-filled and traffic-free Place de la Liberation. Need to walk off that lunch? Then the Jardin de l’Arquebuse, Dijon’s Botanical Gardens, is a lovely place for a relaxing stroll and situated at the edge of the Gardens is the Natural History Museum (free entry).

Weather permitting, you can rent bikes or zip wire through the trees (age 6 and up) at Parc de la Colombieres, which also has farm animals and a jungle gym.

Fancy something very different? In nearby Alesia you can visit the new Gallo-Roman Museum which provides children’s audio guides in several languages, re-enactments from both the Gaul and Roman perspectives and the chance to try out sword fighting.  If your children are too small to appreciate the history of Vercingetorix, you can check them into the free crèche filled with toys and craft activities.

Where to stay

Dijon is a popular place to rent a holiday home, and you can find everything from small apartments to country houses for rent here. In town, the Appartement Jeannin & Antiquaires comes highly recommended.

The Black Forest

It’s long been a popular destination and it’s easy to see why. Magical nature parks, abandoned mines, fairytale castles and of course, black forest cake are all great attractions.

To see indigenous wildlife visit the Steinwasen Adventure Park near Freiburg, also home to a 218 metre long rope bridge, rides and other attractions. Need more animals? Try the Mundenhof Zoo(also in Freiburg). There’s also an interesting alternative bear sanctuary and wolf park in Schapbach.

Since the Black Forest is such a big place stretching from Baden-Baden (so good they named it twice) to Freiburg there isn’t time to list everything, but some highlights include: numerous thermal springs, a trip to a Grimm fairytale castle Berg Hohenzollern and any number of silver mines (for example, the mine in Mount Schauinsland region means taking Germany’s longest cable car to reach the entrance). And the region isn’t short of hotels or holiday homes so you’re sure to find somewhere, even last minute.

If you are planning to go to the Black Forest in the spring, Europa Park (open from April) is a rival to Disneyland, but with slightly cheaper prices and a few less Princesses. It’s themed on countries in Europe (although not all of them) and has rides, shows and other attractions to keep children big and small happy.

Disneyland Paris

It’s just over 3 hours’ drive to Marne-la-Vallée where you’ll find Disneyland Paris’ Magic Kingdom and a vast number of hotels to suit all budgets, both within and outside of the Disney complex. The main reason to stay in-complex is the “magic hours” (their words not mine) you get before the park is open to everyone else. The new Villages Nature (a Disney meets Centreparcs village) has a vast indoor/outdoor swimming pool with several water slides, a farm and treetop adventure park. It is served by a regular bus to Disneyland and the train station plus has a bus service to the Charles de Gaul airport.

Have fun meeting a princess, being a Pirate of the Caribbean, flying a rebel spacecraft in StarTours or free-falling in the Tower of Terror. You can fast track certain rides to get timed tickets which means you don’t have to queue. Entry tickets are not cheap, but the website has current offers here, and you should shop around on group discount sites.

You are also close enough to Paris to take in some of the city’s sights. The station at Marne-la-Vallée runs services to the heart of Paris (approx 40 minutes one way) for a quick stop at the Louvre or the Eiffel Tower.

Bitburg to Bonn

This picturesque part of Germany is home to pretty towns like Bad Munstereifel, a beautiful medieval walled town, in addition to a volcano park, wildlife centres and fairytale castles.

The 12th Century Eltz Castle sits on a secluded hilltop and is still owned by the same family, 33 generations on. Tours in English leave every hour. North of Bitburg the Daun Wildlife Park invites you to drive the 8km dirt road through the woods to visit donkeys, buffalo, llama, ostriches and wild boar. The monkey sanctuary let’s you get up close and personal to our cousins, and there is an enormous playground equipped with trampolines and a reasonably priced restaurant.

Science enthusiasts will enjoy the Volcano Park in the Eifel region with exhibits on volcanoes, archaeology and industrial history as well as educational trails. The museum incorporates 25 stations which can be visited by road, cycle or foot. These include the Roman mine, the spectacular Andernach geyser and the Mendig lava dome. Interactive exhibits bring out the little scientist in your children.

Much of Bonn was destroyed during the Second World War, but there are still some historical remains and the fantastic German National Museum of Contemporary History. Willy Brandt’s car, pieces of the Berlin Wall, and a tiny fragment of rock from the moon are among the 7,000 objects on display.

Where to stay

There are numerous local guesthouses where you can stay in a traditional timber house, and Landal ecoparks have family cabins in Eiffel and Wirfttal if you fancy a place with a pool and other facilities.

The Saar region

So close it’s easy to make a day trip, but why not relax and take longer. The beautiful town of Saarburg provides a lovely setting for a relaxed lunch and walk past the waterfall, or a cable car trip to the nearby hilltop and an all-weather toboggan ride. The castle was built by Count Siegfried of Luxembourg in 946, and you can still walk around the ruins on a hill overlooking the town.

In nearby Merzig, the free Wolfpark Werner Freund is home to several species of wolves from Europe and the Americas, and offers numerous hiking paths through the forests. In the Roscheider Hof Folklore and Open Air Museum in Konz, you can explore everyday life from the regions of Eifel, Hunsrück, Saargau and parts of Lorraine and Luxembourg. There’s a 1912 classroom and an exhibitions on everything from dentistry to wine growing. Mettlach is home to the Villeroy and Boch museum and Völklinger Hütte, formerly old blast furnaces but now a UNESCO heritage site in Völklingen houses an art gallery and science centre focussing on the four elements.

If sightseeing is starting to drag, visit Saarbrucken for some cut-price retail therapy at stores like Primark and TK Maxx, then wave a quick hello to the lemurs and meerkats at and the Saarbrucken Zoo or take a pony ride at Wildpark Saarbrücken.

Where to stay

Saarburg has several hotels and holiday apartments, and a Landal holiday park (slightly run down) next to the cable car stop, whilst nearby Centre Parcs Bostalsee, set by a pretty lake has a swimming pool with toboggan rides, a mini farm and other activities.
