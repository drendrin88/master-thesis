Luxembourg Autofestival – tips for buying a car

As it does at this time every year, Luxembourg's 10-day Autofestival  starts on 26 January

sarita rao
26.01.2019— Last update: 26.01.2019, 09:17

The 55th Autofestival, organised by ADAL and FEGARLUX will run from 26 January until 4 February 2019 with some 75 garages taking part and a total cumulative showroom space of 60,000m2.

If you are thinking of buying a new car or a second hand one, this is the time to do it. Car dealers will have special promotions and discounts and most banks offer festival loan and lease terms at discounted rates. Even if you're not in the market for a new car, there will be new models to test drive, so you can check out the latest automotive technology.

Last year, a record 52,811 new cars were registered in Luxembourg, a third record year with sales at their highest. Some 18,000 orders were taken at the Autofestival which this year expects in excess of 30,000 visitors from the Greater Region.

Here's some information on car choices, bank loans and employee discounts, and buying a used car, to help you navigate Autofestival.

Petrol, diesel or electric?

In fact the choice is even greater now, with electric, plug-in hybrids, natural gas and hydrogen technology. Most car buyers choose between petrol and diesel, and there is a comparison table of the pros and cons, but electric and hybrid are increasingly becoming a popular environmentally friendly choice. This year the tests for the next Euro emissions standards will begin with a likely impact on some types of vehicles from 2020 onwards.

You can receive a tax allowance for zero-emission personal vehicles that are fully powered by electricity or a hydrogen fuel cell of €5,000 and one for €2,500 for plug-in hybrids whose emissions do not exceed 50g CO2/km. The Autofestival website provides prospective purchasers with a calculator for car tax and information about vehicle registration.

The truth is that car buying is no longer a simple choice. Smaller models can hide powerful engines, it is possible to buy cheap 4 x 4s and some brands of electric car can run for 600km without needing to be charged. Online advice suggests you fix your budget, then list your car criteria in order of importance – make, model, fuel consumption, size, essential equipment, and the main use such as commuting to work or family travel. Finally, test drive more than one car to get a comparison, ideally on a route you’ll be driving regularly so you can try out road holding, manoeuvrability, acceleration and comfort.

Cash or credit?

With low interest rates on savings it might be more economical to combine cash savings with a car loan.

Many banks in Luxembourg offer unique Autofestival car purchase loan or lease terms so it's worth shopping around, and also asking the dealer for their terms.

Bank loans for buying or leasing

Car loans offer very attractive rates compared to personal loans because the vehicle itself constitutes a guarantee to the bank. Loans are usually paid back through fixed monthly instalments.

Banks provide online calculators so you can work out your monthly payments including interest over the period of time you want to repay your loan and see the total amount in interest you will pay in addition to the cost of the vehicle.

For example, with an AER rate of 2.97% a car costing €27,000 will require a monthly payment of €596.84 over a 48 month (4 year) period. The interest you will pay over this period is approximately €1648, but interest is tax deductable.

0% credit loans

Car dealers will often offer 0% interest loans. There's no such thing as a free lunch, so the dealer will be covering the cost of the loan and may limit the duration, meaning you have very high monthly payments and a big first down payment.

Check too, that a loan doesn't negate any other discounts on the car which might outweigh the benefit of 0% interest on a loan. Basically compare the savings you get from an interest free loan against the savings you might lose on discounts or the trade in of your old car.

Employee discounts

Your company might offer discounts with specific dealers (particularly if it runs a fleet of company cars) or with car loan providers. In addition, those working for the EU institutions can benefit from a VAT (TVA) exemption from the purchase of a car valued above the VAT threshold (both new and used).

Buying a used car

New cars lose value the minute they drive from the forecourt, so sometimes a used car can be better value for money, depending on its age and history. Think about the market depreciation value of the brand of car you are choosing. Some brands like BMW and VW depreciate at a slower rate.

At the dealers, make sure you read through the vehicle maintenance records, and check invoices, registration card, roadworthiness test documents and the log book. As a rule of thumb, things start to need fixing or replacing when vehicles are over 5 years old with mileage of more than 100,000km, so it’s worth noting if a car has been serviced regularly.

Inspect the exterior paintwork, windows and windscreen, tyres and wipers. Inside the car check the speedometer, seats and seat covers, dashboard controls and air conditioning. Cars that have been sitting idle at the showroom may need a battery and air conditioner unit recharge. Ideally you should take a test drive, to hear the sound of the engine, check the gearbox and brakes and watch for any leakages after the car has been driven, such as transmission fluid. It's worth also checking the exhaust for emissions, as a car can fail its contrôle technique (MOT) on this. If you prefer the advice of a professional, ACL can do a check for you. Foyer also provides advice and a checklist for buying a nearly-new car.

Before you transfer payment, make sure you have all the necessary documents including an invoice. Insurer Foyer provides invoice templates.

Cross-border car purchasing

It is possible to buy your car over the border and register it in Luxembourg (the same process if you bring a car from your home country and need to register it). The European Consumer Centre Luxembourghas some information on this, and the Luxembourg government provides information on car registration and roadworthiness certificates.
